Ahmad's Book Recommendations

The Open Society and Its Enemies by Karl Popper

When Erik Kandel went on a limb to study memory by analyzing neural processes in the giant snail Aplysia, Karl Popper encouraged him. But he didn't encourage Kandel by telling him that he was going to be right. The nature of Popper's encouragement captures his attitude towards science.

Aplysia has few neurons, but they are large, so they are easier to study. When Kandel was researching them, almost all of his colleagues believed that you could not extrapolate learnings from non-complex systems, like giant snails, to complex ones, like humans. Kandel thought that it might be possible, although almost everyone told him that he'd be wasting his time.

Popper told Kandel that he should try and run head on into the face of failure. In Popper's view, Kandel's job as a scientist was not to be right. His job was to make sure that beliefs were grounded in evidence, and there was limited evidence that non-complex systems could not inform the study of complex ones. Kandel ran into the face of failure, and as luck would have it, there was no failure on the other end. There was a Nobel prize. (Read In Search of Memory by Erik Kandel to learn more about Kandel's work and his recollection of his interactions with Popper.)

In The Open Society, Popper carefully dismantles political ideas that make us more inclined to believe in unproven positives instead of acknowledging the true limitations of our knowledge. It's a remarkable and inspiring demonstration of Popper's own analytical ability, which allowed him to see past the biases that exist and outline a concise attack on long-standing traditions of political philosophy.

To Kill a Mockingbird by Harper Lee

A powerful novel on racism and justice in the American South, this book remains as relevant today as ever. The characters are unforgettable, especially Atticus Finch.

1984 by George Orwell

A haunting look at a dystopian future where totalitarianism reigns supreme. Orwell's vision is a chilling reminder of the dangers of unchecked power.
